History of Desto Finger Caw Cawter Oli: Ethiopia’s Adaptive Game

In Ethiopia, there's a cultural tendency to reshape foreign words and ideas, making them uniquely Ethiopian. For instance, the word "congratulations" is commonly shortened to "congra," and the name "Bob Marley" is often referred to as "Bomb Marly." This cultural adaptability extends to everything, including music, language, and even games.

During the five-year Italian occupation of Ethiopia (1936–1941), this ability to reshape and adapt foreign influences led to the transformation of a simple just finger can count song into the game Desto Finger Caw Cawter Oli. The song, originally in English, was converted into an Ethiopian version, and the game itself was invented during the early years of the occupation by combining the just finger can count song and the Italian hand game Morra which has roots in ancient Roman and Greek times. This fusion of elements created something new yet familiar, reflecting the blend of cultures.

The Influence of Morra

Morra is a hand game in which players throw out any number of fingers and try to guess the total sum of fingers shown by both players. It’s a game of quick thinking and luck, popular in Italy for centuries. When the Italians occupied Ethiopia, they brought Morra with them, and it quickly caught on. However, true to Ethiopian culture, the game wasn’t simply adopted; it was transformed into something uniquely Ethiopian.

The Ethiopian Adaptation Desto Finger Caw Cawter Oli

Ethiopians took the structure of Morra and blended it with the rhythmic elements of a just finger can count song. In Morra, players called out numbers, but in the Ethiopian version, players instead choose from five rhythmic phrases: Desto, Finger, Caw, Cawter, and Oli. This shift from numbers to phrases gave the game a distinctly local flavor, reflecting the way Ethiopians adapt foreign influences to fit their culture, much like the way they change words like “congratulations” to “congra.”

How to Play

Desto Finger Caw Cawter Oli is a two-player game with flexible rules that allow players to choose one or two variables, depending on what they agree upon. The rules are as follows

01

Choosing Variables

Each player selects one or two phrases from the set: Desto, Finger, Caw, Cawter, or Oli. For instance, Player 1 might choose Desto and Finger, while Player 2 selects Caw and Cawter.

02

Random Finger Throw

Once the variables are chosen, both players throw out a random number of fingers, typically between zero and ten.

03

Counting the Phrases

The players then count from Desto through Oli, matching the number of fingers shown. The count always follows the order: Desto, Finger, Caw, Cawter, Oli

04

Winning the Round

If the final count matches one of the chosen phrases, the player who selected that phrase wins. For example, if the count stops at Cawter and Player 2 had chosen Cawter, they win the round.

05

Tip

The right combination can be the key to victory, so think ahead and plan your moves carefully!.
